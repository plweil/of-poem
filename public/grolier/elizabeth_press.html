  45. Elizabeth Press Catalogs

    Two catalogues, for 1972 and 1978/9. The 1972 catalogue includes an interesting essay by Weil, “The Amenities of Book-Buying,” that closes with the statement that the ultimate amenity of book-buying lay in the belief that “only after a book has been read is it complete and completely one’s own.”

  46. Elizabeth Magazine

    Two issues of a small literary magazine edited and published by Weil in the 1960s, Elizabeth V (March 1963) and Elizabeth VII (March 1964). They contain poems by many of the poets that were published by the Elizabeth Press, including Theodore Enslin, Gena Ford, Barriss Mills, John Judson, Frederick Eckman, Felix Stefanile, and R.R. Cuscaden. Note the subtitle on the cover of Elizabeth: “a magazine of Elizabethan and Metaphysical poetry.”
